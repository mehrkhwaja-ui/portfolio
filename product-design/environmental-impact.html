My Impact ✦

Quantifying the carbon costs of bank visits

Real product walkthrough, screen recorded.

How do you make someone feel good about choosing an app over driving to a bank branch? I designed a feature that quantified the invisible: the fuel, time, and carbon emissions customers were already saving by banking digitally.

USER RESEARCH | DATA VISUALIZATION | BEHAVIORAL DESIGN

01 Challenge

The bank wanted to migrate more customers to digital channels to cut costs. But guilting people into behavior change doesn't work. So I flipped the script: what if, instead of pushing people to switch, we helped customers who were already banking digitally see, and feel proud of, the impact they were already making?

02 Research

I surveyed 120 customers to understand their branch habits: average distance traveled, number of transactions completed per visit, and typical mode of transport. I paired that with external data, average petrol prices, vehicle ownership rates in Pakistan, and CO2 emissions per litre of fuel, and worked with the branch team to pull real queue time data from their Q matic system.

That let me calculate, per transaction, what a customer avoids by banking digitally instead of visiting a branch.

03 Solution

The My Impact feature shows customers what percentage of their transactions happened digitally over the past 30 days, then translates that into money, fuel, and carbon they avoided spending by not driving to a branch. It closes with a soft prompt: "You're already doing so much. Want to help a bit more?", inviting customers to plant a tree or donate to an environmental cause.

Watch the walkthrough alongside.

04 Impact

The feature helped the bank win the AsiaMoney Environmental, Social and Governance award, and helped shift the bank's relationship with customers from purely transactional to values aligned, giving people a reason to feel good about a habit they'd already built.

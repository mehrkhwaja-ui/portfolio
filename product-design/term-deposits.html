Product Design — UBL

Term Deposits: making investing feel safe for people who'd never invested before.

A digital investment feature that turned a jargon-heavy, branch-only process into a plain-language journey — bringing PKR 7 billion onto the bank's investment portfolio in its first year.

Role
Product Designer
Company
UBL
Timeline
2021 – 2024
Skills
UX Research, Interaction Design, Financial Literacy

01 Challenge

Investing in Pakistan was something people associated with being older, wealthier, and already financially fluent. Most investment products lived entirely at the branch, and where they did exist digitally, the process was usually just the jargon-laden branch flow copy-pasted onto a screen. Younger, first-time bankers and less financially savvy customers steered clear — not because they didn't want to invest, but because the product made them feel out of their depth.

UBL's mandate went beyond digitizing the branch experience: we wanted to humanize it.

02 Research

I interviewed two groups: people interested in investing who had never actually done it, and existing investment customers who had gone through the branch process. Across both groups, I collected the pain points and points of confusion that made investing feel inaccessible — unfamiliar terminology, unclear risk, and a process that assumed prior knowledge the customer didn't have.

Those findings became the brief: strip the jargon, explain every term in plain language, and build trust at every step — especially for customers using UBL's Roshan Digital Account, many of whom were investing with the bank for the first time from abroad.

03 Solution

The result was the Mahana Aamdani Term Deposit flow — a guided, plain-language journey from Wealth Management entry point through tenure selection, account selection, payment, and confirmation. Every screen was designed to explain rather than assume: what a term deposit is, what happens to your money, and when you'll see it again.

04 Why Now

Digital-first customers, especially those using Roshan Digital Accounts from abroad, wanted a way to grow their savings without a branch visit or a phone call to a relationship manager. Positioning term deposits as an approachable, self-serve product met that demand directly — and gave UBL a way to deepen relationships with customers who were otherwise purely transactional.

05 Impact

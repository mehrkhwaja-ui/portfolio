The Graveyard ▲

Exploring how censorship breeds new slang

What happens when you can't say what you mean online? You invent new language. "Unalive" for suicide. "Leg boobies" for thighs. "Camping" for concentration camps. In May 2025, my group and I built an interactive archive of algospeak, the coded language people create to evade censorship on platforms like TikTok, Weibo, and YouTube. The site reveals how language contorts to survive, and who benefits from that contortion.

The Three Sections

The Graveyard: explore banned words from mainland China, the Philippines, and TikTok US, visualized as forgotten file cabinets. Click to see how political resistance reshapes syntax itself.

The New World: the dark side, how QAnon adherents and incels weaponize euphemisms to spread extremism under the radar of content moderation. (Warning: disturbing content.)

The Overlord: a speculative tool where visitors can type sensitive words and receive algorithmically optimized alternatives. A playful provocation: what if we had to optimize our language for machines instead of humans?
